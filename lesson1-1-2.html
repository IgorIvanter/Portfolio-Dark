Nouns.
Articles.

Introduction

First, let's figure out what nouns are. Nouns are words denoting any objects or concepts, for example: table, dog, weather. Nouns always answer the question Who? or What?

German nouns can be either masculine, feminine or neutral. There's no clear rules on how to determine which of those a given word is. You will have to remember it for every word you learn.

Articles

In most cases, an article is placed before a German noun. Most often in German sentences you will find not just the words Auto, Mann, Stadt, etc., but ein Auto, ein Mann, eine Stadt

There are two types of articles: indefinite and definite.

The indefinite article is used when we are talking about some object that is still unknown to us. Maybe it will be a little unclear for now, but when we say "I have a table", we mean a table in general, not the same table that we have already seen, but just some kind of table that we are talking about for the first time.

Ich habe einen Tisch.
I have a table.

In this case masculine and neutral words will have the article ein and feminine the article eine.

Definite article. We should use such an article when we are talking about a specific subject that is already known to us or to the interlocutor. For example, if we say "the table is in the kitchen", then we will mean a specific table already.

Der Tisch ist in der KÃ¼che
The table is in the kitchen

The definite article also depends on the noun. Masculine nouns will have the article der, neutral das and feminine die.

The usage of definite and indefinite articles is the same as the usage of the and a/an in Egnlish.
